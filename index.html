Summary

General

The following factors were established in the Web Proposal in the previous assignment.

OBJECTIVE:
It is important to first revise the objective of this new site: provide information. The purpose of thie site is that it is informative - it is content-rich. Thorough research - from Mental Health Foundation Australia itself and many other secondary sources - was conducted to gather information that populates the site. Ultimately, learning through subtle interactivity is the primary call-to-action.

TARGET AUDIENCE:
This remains as two major age groups - Young Persons (15-24 years old) and Middle-Aged (45-54 years old), as determined in user research. They are the two groups most vulnerable to mental health illnesses. - Information is catered to them specifically. For example, in “Who to Approach”, the organisations listed are specific to their needs, and the websites that are linked externally have these two parties as their own target audience. This will be explained further later.

FORM:
The aesthetic of the page, despite it being an informative page, is to be kept as minimalistic. Therefore many words are hidden from user’s main view unless hovered or clicked. There was also an attempt to keep a balanced and symmetrical organisation of information, with a line about centre y-axis. The corners of all containers and images are also rounded. With less sharp edges, I wanted to make the site more welcoming and friendly, particularly so for its purpose as a mental health site.

COLOUR:
The colour scheme follows the palette determined in the proposal - purple, blue and yellow, or split complementary colour palette. This aims to encourage easy-reading but also convey hope to individuals struggling with mental health.

MEDIA QUERIES:
The site was coded for an iPhone X first, then scaled up to the desktop size, compatible with a Macbook Pro.

The process of developing the 4 pages is as follows:

Landing Page > About Us Page > Information Page > Information Sub Page

Landing Page

The Landing Page is key to showing the options that the user can select. Therefore, the icons, showing the information (and hence subpages) are the key focus. The graphical icons are created using bright colours and a fun design, so as to encourage users to select them.

    Key Points:
  • Home and Emergency Contact to be stayed throughout all pages - so that user can access the home (landing) page and make an emergency call if needed (feeling overwhelmed).
  • The footer, which shows a quote that promotoes hope, will also be displayed throughout all the pages.
  • Comparing the Previous and Final Landing Pages, there was a slight change (iteration). Through user feedback, it was understood that the thinner, single-lined border is not strong enough to accentuate the organisation. Because of its thinness, it was also prompting user to select an element within the container.
  • The border is then changed to a thicker, double-lined border. The double border acts as a restriction - like a double barrier or double yellow line on the roads - user is less likley to select an element within the page.
  • Border is dotted around all icons to indicate that the user can possibly select them. This differentiates them from the top header graphic.
  • Hovering causes text to change colour, indicating to the user that it can be selected. Not only the text is selectable - its container too. This gives space for users to make mistakes.
  • Transitions: The graphic headers in the individual subpages (e.g. the graphics at the top of About Us and Information pages) are matched to the icons on the Landing Page. This gives continuity and let the user better understand that he selected that icon/page previously.

"About Us" Page

The About Us Page is important to showcasing facts about the organisation. Often, it is mislooked by viewers because they are more concerned over the content that the site will be showing. However, I felt that it was important to showcase how this organisation is different from other organisations.

    Key Points:
  • Information about Mental Health Foundation Australia was sieved out carefully, particularly the objectives they wish to promote and the members on the board.
  • Images of members of the board are added to match faces with names - making the information easier to relate to, and friendlier!
  • I felt that it was important to showcase these information and how they are different from other organisations. The interesting fact is that Mental Health Foundation is the oldest establishment that aims to advocate mental wellbeing in Australia! Therefore, its history was summarised briefly.
  • Just like the previous Landing Page, there was an attempt to centralise the content about the central y-axis, further emphasising the importance of balance.

"Information" Page

This page could be described as a “selection page”. Users can click and then be directed directly to the information that they want in another subsequent page.

    Key Points:
  • This Information Page is kept minimalistic. For a start, it has three square graphic blocks. In this above scenario, only the Young Person's graphic is added in as a prototype.
  • When users hover over the blocks, the text container will appear, allowing user to make a selection.
  • This made use of a href tags in the lists, contained in the text containers.
  • When the mouse hovers over the text, the colour changes to indicate that the words are clickable. The colour of the hovered-over text also follows the colour theme of the site, but also ensuring that it caters to those who may be colour blind. This improves accessibility.

"Information Sub" Page

This page is where the bulk of the content and information will be. This is the most developed page. As mentioned at the beginning, the objective of this site is to help users learn. Therefore, facts and figures are important to encorurage that.

    Key Points:
  • This Information Sub Page is also minimalistic too. Despite having a lot of information, they are kept away from sight in their own containers unless an event happens.
  • I was inspired by flash cards - where in real life, users will flip cards over to reveal the answers. This also ensures that information is disgestible. Because flash cards are often limited in physical space, I wanted to display information to educate, but kept succinct.
  • When users hover over the blocks, the text container will appear, allowing user to make a selection.
  • This made use of a href tags in the lists, contained in the text containers.
  • I wanted users to view only one piece of information at once, therefore only having access to one container at a time. Therefore, :hover was used the most across the page.
  • Colour-coding was used, based on the majority of the colour used for icon selection graphic in the previous page. For example, blue was used the most for the young, and orange was used for the middle-aged. Subsequently, purple will be used for carers.
  • As seen in the above two screenshots, the content displayed for Young Persons (15 to 24 years old) and the Middle-Aged (45 to 54 years old) do differ. For example:
    • The amount of text displayed for Young Persons is significantly less. This is to cater to an audience with shorter attention span, who are less likely to reading big amount of texts.
    • Because of the longer text shown for the Middle-Aged, bolding of keywords was used to showcase more important information. This helps make the information skimmable as well.

Process

This section explains how the website is created, in more detail.

Step 1

As established in the Web Proposal, the Futura font was best suited for the theme of the site. It was also important to code an extra "backup" font, just in case the browser does not support the Futura font, or if any other problems arise.

Step 2

The navigation bars for the top (i.e. Home and Emergency Contact) and the content were created at this point. The a href tags were used to link between the different pages.

Step 3

After the first experimentation of the styling of the navigation bars, the colour palette for the site was used to change the background and text colour of bars.

Step 4

The graphics used for the top header of every page and in directory for content were created in Adobe Illustrator. Hence, the assets were then exported in SVG format, so that they are compatible to the resizing of the browser (responsiveness).

Step 5

Once the assets were imported into my own local folder, it is then imported into Visual Studio Code (VSC) so that they can be used later. The graphics were first trialed using the Landing Page.
Note: the above graphic was later replaced using the logo of the Mental Health Foundation Australia because it better protrayed this site as a representative of the organisation.

Step 6

As explained earlier, the graphic icons were the key focus of the Landing Page. Therefore, multiple div tags were created - with a shared class - so that they can be styled simultaneously. Because the graphic icons and titles were housed in their own containers, it was useful in coding and allowing users to select any part of this entire container to be directed. This allows for user error in selection.

Step 7

Because the purpose of the site was to give information, I felt that it was important to showcase the general statistics of the mental health status in Australia. This led on to the concluding statement of "You are not alone.", so as to encourage users to step forth and be part of a community. In the same vein, a footer was created to push forth the idea of courage and recovery. This quote was written by Shakespeare, and it showed in a simple line, the power of one's own will.

Step 8

The HTML and CSS for the Information Page was created, alongside a general format of the page using div tags. Since the navigation bars and footer were to be consistent across all the pages, their codes (both HTML and CSS) were copied over from the Landing Page.

Step 9

The top graphic header of this Information Page, that is similar to that of graphic icon in the previous Landing Page, was added. This gives a quick introduction the page even before the user looks at the general word-written content.

Step 10

The previous version saw that the graphic icon and the text container be presented in two columns. However, feedback was given on how this may not be the best for user experience, particularly for users who are older and may not be able to see clerly.

Using the feedback given, the graphic icons and text containers are displayed in a single block column instead. This was for ease of reading and selection.

Step 11

The text container was given a dotted border. This was consistent with the style that was used on the Landing Page. The text was also a blue colour to give a good contrast with the white background.

Step 12

For the possibility of differing styling and organisation, the content for Young Persons, Middle-Aged and Carers were put into different sections.

Step 13

For each part of the information (i.e. General, Common Types of Struggles, How to Overcome Them and Who to Approach), they were put into a bigger container using div tags. This was also for better organisations of information.

Step 14

Each part of the information (i.e. General, Common Types of Struggles, How to Overcome Them and Who to Approach) are now given their own unique id tag. This was done, so that in the future, if the style for each card is to be different, it can be coded.

Step 15

As explained earlier, as I wanted the the information to be presented like flash cards, the hover function was used across the individual containers (with the id tags).

Step 16

For Young Persons (15 to 24 years old), content nested inside the flash cards is less.

Step 17

The display format for the Middle-Aged age group was repeated from that of the Young Persons age group.

Step 18

For Middle-Aged (45 to 54 years old), facts and figures are key to be persuasive. This appeals to their Logos, or logical reasoning. However, keywords were still bolded to ensure that they are still convenient and efficient to read.

Step 19

Once most content was settled, media queries was added to make the website responsive. In this project, it will be for the iPhone X and the Macbook Pro.

Step 20

Media queries were added across all other pages. They were added in this order: Information Sub Page, Information Page, Landing Page and About Us Page. This was determined based on the priority of these pages.

Future Improvements and Development

  • Generate information for Carers
  • Make use of CSS or Javascript animations instead of merely hover effects
  • Make use of :active - make information stay on page so user can look across and compare multiple pieces of information
  • Make use of Javascript code - code more responsive sites with better event functions
  • Develop a Blog Page - give more information
  • Develop a Log In/Register Page as the second Call-to-Action - users can make appointments or participate in community events
  • Develop a Find Support Page - users can connect to online platforms or choose to attend events for their own age groups
